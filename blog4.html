My perspective on unemployment in Bhutan

• Mar 15, 2024 • 10 min read

Where is Laya?

Ah, Laya! The mere mention of its name conjures images of mist-covered mountains, yak-filled pastures, and the warm smiles of its resilient inhabitants. My week-long trek to this remote village in Bhutan was nothing short of an adventure of epic proportions, filled with laughter, sweat, and the occasional bout of altitude-induced delirium.

As I embarked on the journey, I quickly realized that my idea of "packing light" was a tad optimistic. Struggling under the weight of my overstuffed backpack, I couldn't help but wonder if I had packed my entire life's belongings instead of just a week's worth. Lesson learned: next time, leave the kitchen sink at home.

My Journey

The trail itself was a rollercoaster of emotions and physical challenges. From navigating steep inclines that left me gasping for air to crossing rickety bridges that seemed to sway with every step, each leg of the journey presented its own unique set of obstacles. But amidst the exhaustion and occasional twisted ankle, there were moments of sheer awe and wonder as I beheld the untouched beauty of the Bhutanese wilderness.

Arriving in Laya felt like stumbling upon a hidden oasis in the midst of a vast desert. The village, with its colorful wooden houses perched on the mountainside, felt like something out of a fairy tale. And the people! Oh, the people of Laya welcomed us with open arms, their laughter and warmth melting away any lingering fatigue.

Final Thoughts

But perhaps the most memorable part of my time in Laya was the sense of connection I felt with both nature and my fellow trekkers. As we sat around the communal fire, swapping stories and sharing our hopes and fears, I couldn't help but feel grateful for the opportunity to experience such a magical place with such wonderful companions.

So here's to Laya, a place where the air is thin but the spirits are high, where every step is a journey and every moment is a treasure. Until we meet again, may your mountains be ever majestic and your yaks ever friendly.
